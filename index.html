About Me

Researcher. Engineer.
Builder of robots that think.

I'm completing my Masters in Robotic Systems Development (MRSD) at Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute, graduating May 2026 with a 3.99 GPA. I'm a Research Assistant in the Bot Intelligence Group under Dr. Jean Oh, where I contribute to RIO — a flexible real-time Robot I/O framework for unified sensor ingestion, teleoperation, and policy execution across robot embodiments (under review at RSS 2026).

My work spans high-frequency teleoperation pipelines (50–80 Hz), multi-modal teleoperation interfaces (VR controllers, SpaceMouse, master–slave manipulators), and evaluating Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies — including π0.5 and GR00T — on real robot systems. I'm currently extending this work to Unitree G1 humanoid robots.

My active research project is multimodal pedestrian intent prediction for autonomous driving — fusing visual features with text-based intent prompts and designing attention mechanisms that ground fine-grained cues like gaze and posture in symbolic traffic context. I recently completed my Lunar ROADSTER capstone under Dr. William "Red" Whittaker, building the full autonomy stack for a lunar-grading rover with 89% crater detection accuracy across 100+ hours of field testing.

Before CMU, I spent a year at Yulu Bikes in Bangalore building real-time state estimation and ML-based fall-prediction systems for electric micro-mobility, and led the autonomous driving software stack at Orion Racing India for Formula SAE. I'm looking for full-time roles in robot learning, embodied AI, and autonomous systems.

03 / Optimal Control

Model-Based Control for Humanoid Soccer

QP + trajectory optimization for target-directed kicking on Unitree G1

  • Developed a model-based control framework for humanoid soccer kicking in the Unitree G1 simulation environment, integrating whole-body planning with accurate ball-strike execution
  • Formulated a Quadratic Program (QP) to compute optimal foot impulse and contact point for target-directed ball strikes, with Direct Collocation trajectory optimization for dynamically feasible kick paths
  • Implemented IHLQR and TVLQR for whole-body balance stabilization and accurate trajectory tracking throughout the kicking motion, handling ground-contact transitions
